Q&A Section - Bhagavad Gita

Q: What is the Bhagavad Gita?

A: The Bhagavad Gita is a 700-verse Hindu scripture that is part of the Mahabharata, offering spiritual guidance through a dialogue between Krishna and Arjuna.

Q: Who is the author of the Bhagavad Gita?

A: The Bhagavad Gita is traditionally attributed to the sage Vyasa, who also compiled the Mahabharata.

Q: What is the main message of the Bhagavad Gita?

A: The Gita emphasizes performing one’s duty (dharma) without attachment to results, focusing on selfless action, devotion, and spiritual wisdom.

Q: Why should we read the Bhagavad Gita?

A: Reading the Gita helps individuals understand life’s purpose, find inner peace, and achieve self-realization, offering timeless wisdom applicable to daily life.

Q: What are the paths to liberation mentioned in the Gita?

A: The Gita describes three main paths to liberation: Karma Yoga (selfless action), Bhakti Yoga (devotion), and Jnana Yoga (knowledge).

Q: How does the Gita address fear?

A: The Gita teaches that fear arises from attachment and ignorance. By realizing the eternal nature of the soul, one can overcome fear.
